What it actually does

The auditor reads a bill (PDF, image, or text), then runs three passes:

  • Parser: extracts every line item, code, charge, adjustment, and patient-responsibility figure.
  • Auditor: checks for upcoding (inflated CPT codes), unbundling (charges that should be combined), duplicates, missing contractual adjustments, illegal balance billing, and abusive facility fees.
  • Dispute writer: if the audit finds critical issues, it drafts a formal dispute letter quoting the bill back at the provider, citing the federal or state law that applies, and naming where to mail it.

It also includes a charity care eligibility checker — nonprofit hospitals are required by IRS Section 501(r) to write off bills for patients below specific income thresholds. Most patients never know they qualify.
